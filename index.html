Educational History

Being raised in Esterhazy, I took elementary school at P.J. Gillen School and high school in Esterhazy High School. It was there that I first took Computer Science (as well as Communications Media). In high school, I learned my first few programming languages; Java, Visual Basic, and HTML. I also learned how to use Excel and Access there. I took Computer Science every year that I could, and the fact that I got good marks in it really showed that you tend to do better at things that you enjoy!

After high school, I entered the Computer Systems Technology program in Sask Polytech's Regina Campus. At first I mainly was shaking off rust from high school, but soon enough I was learning about operating appliances... the list goes on. For Year 2 of CST I moved to Saskatoon (transfering to Saskpoly's Saskatoon Campus) and continued learning about more IT sectors (like security, and working in collaborative teams for full-stack development projects... basically just a bunch of programmers working together to make a website that can do all sorts of things). My resume goes more in-depth on the projects I've worked on. So far I haven't found a subject that I don't want to know more about. I like trying to replicate some of the things that I learn in school, like setting up multiple computers inside my computer (aka virtual machines), building my own computer (something that I take way too much pride in) and coding my own side projects (you could probably consider this website one of them). I intend to expand my IT skills across several sectors, so I'm never entirely out of my element.

About Me

Ever since high school Computer Science (and Communications Media), I've been fascinated by the things that computers are capable of. I took Computer Science every year that I could, and the fact that I got good marks in it really showed that you tend to do better at things that you enjoy! My high school Computer Science teacher was my idol growing up, so in the end I chose to pursue a career in the high school subject that I took the most pride in. Saskpoly has really brightened my horizons in terms of what you do in each sector of IT. Of course, as you might tell, I take the most joy (thus far) in programming and web development, as every other greenhorn IT student does. What I think makes me different from other students is that my curiosity towards IT is more... generalized. I want to know a little bit about everything. My high school teacher was my idol growing up, and so not only do I want to be a tech guru similar to him, but I want to have the industry experience to one day be the senior IT guy anybody from any sector can come to with questions. That's my ambition. I'm still enrolled in Saskpoly, and in a few short months (from the moment I write this), I plan to continue my studies at the University of Saskatchewan.

Several Java Projects

I studied Java in both high school and Saskpoly, so it's the language that I have the most experience in, but because I'm mostly a programmer, it's also naturally the language that I dread the most (because I've seen just about every error it can throw). Java has been a stepping stone towards my mastery of Object-Oriented Programming. However, more recently I've been working with its sibling lanuage C# and their predecessor C. With that being said, Java's flexibility as a language (in terms of what it can run on) is something that still impresses me. It's a very good language to start with. Saskpoly did well by starting its students off with this language, and not Python.
