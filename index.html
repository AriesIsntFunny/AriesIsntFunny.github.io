Educational History

Being raised in Esterhazy, I took elementary school at P.J. Gillen School and high school in Esterhazy High School. It was there that I first took Computer Science (as well as Communications Media). In high school, I learned my first few programming languages; Java, Visual Basic, and HTML. I also learned how to use Excel and Access there. I took Computer Science every year that I could, and the fact that I got good marks in it really showed that you tend to do better at things that you enjoy!

After high school, I entered the Computer Systems Technology program in Sask Polytech's Regina Campus. At first I mainly was shaking off rust from high school, but soon enough I was learning about things like operating systems (specifically Windows 10 and Windows Server in-depth) and networking appliances. For Year 2 of CST I moved to Saskatoon (transfering to Saskpoly's Saskatoon Campus) and continued learning about more IT sectors (like the rabbit hole that is Security, SCCM, Server Manager, and working in collaborative teams for full-stack development projects... basically just a bunch of programmers working together to make a website that can do all sorts of things). My resume goes more in-depth on the projects I've worked on. So far I haven't found a subject that I don't want to know more about. I like trying to replicate some of the things that I learn in school, like setting up multiple computers inside my computer (aka virtual machines), building my own computer (something that I take way too much pride in) and coding my own side projects (you could probably consider this website one of them). I intend to expand my IT skills across several sectors, so I'm never entirely out of my element.

The Big Vue Project (w/ CRUD)

At the end of Year 2 Semester 1, I was tasked with making a complete frontend with Vuejs, and 2 CRUD interfaces. (CRUD meaning it that it gives the user the ability to Create, Read, Update, and Delete something). When I was done, I had one of the projects which I'm the most proud of, because of the sheer amount of work I put in, in such a short amount of time. I used Vuejs, Vuex for communication between Vue components, Bootstrap Vue, and just a basic JSON server for my backend. All in order to make a knockoff Twitter that didn't take itself seriously. But hey, I got marked well on it. Eventually I want to be a seasoned full-stack developer (whether it be professionally or just a hobbyist), so this experience was definitely useful in coming closer to that.


Building A Proposal, Scavenging Hardware

As the final project that was jointed between a Technical Communication class and a Hardware class, we were split into groups and given the assignment to make a hardware proposal for a company that was definitely not just our teacher. Our job was to both find computer parts that satisfy the requirements of the company, and write them into a proposal that was describing what our intention was, why we chose those parts, why we thought they would be the best fit, why they should trust us, and also show that we knew our stuff by incorporating a Table of Contents, Table of Figures, Summary, Formal Introduction, Background, Part Introduction, Product Review for each part, Conclusion... the works. I was the one in the group who handled most of the writing, along with a couple of the parts. My own computer is still far better than what we cooked up with that budget build. Really, I should be incorporating the time and effort I put into building my pc and the trouble I went through into its own part of this website, but that doesn't seem to fit anywhere.
